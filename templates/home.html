Hypertension What it is?

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a condition where the pressure in your blood vessels is consistently elevated. It's diagnosed when your blood pressure is 130/80 millimeters of mercury (mm Hg) or higher, based on the average of two or more readings taken on different occasions. Hypertension is common and can be serious if left untreated.
